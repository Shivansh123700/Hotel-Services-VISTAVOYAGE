what are payment methods?

Credit Cards: Credit cards are widely accepted at hotels. Major credit card brands such as Visa, MasterCard, American Express, and Discover are commonly used. The hotel may request a pre-authorization or place a hold on the card for incidentals. Debit Cards: Debit cards are generally accepted, but there may be some differences in policies. Hotels might place a hold on funds in your account for incidentals, so it's essential to check with the hotel about their specific debit card policies. Cash: We accept cash payments, especially at the time of check-out. However, many hotels prefer card payments, and some may require a credit card for reservation purposes even if you plan to pay with cash at the end of your stay. Mobile Payments: With the increasing popularity of mobile payment apps, some hotels may accept payments through services like Apple Pay, Google Pay, or other digital wallets. Check with the hotel to confirm their acceptance of specific mobile payment methods. Traveler's Checks: While less common than in the past, some hotels may still accept traveler's checks. It's advisable to check with the hotel in advance to ensure they will honor them. Bank Transfers: For certain types of reservations, especially for large groups or events, hotels may accept payments through bank transfers. This is typically arranged in advance with the hotel's accounting or reservations department.

how to claim coupons codes?

Look for a section during the checkout process that allows you to enter a coupon code. This might be labeled as "Promo Code," "Coupon Code,". Enter the coupon code you obtained into the designated field. Double-check to ensure that you've entered it correctly After entering the coupon code, there is usually a button or option to apply it. Click on that button to activate the discount or promotion associated with the coupon

How secure is the hotel?

Surveillance: Many hotels employ surveillance cameras in public areas, entrances, and hallways to monitor and enhance security. Key Card Access: Modern hotels often use key card systems for room access, which can enhance security by limiting entry to authorized guests. Security Personnel: Larger hotels may have on-site security personnel to monitor the premises and respond to any security concerns. Emergency Procedures: Hotels typically have established emergency procedures, including fire evacuation plans and protocols for other potential crises. Room Safes: Many hotels provide in-room safes for guests to secure their valuables. Guest Verification: Front desk staff may require proper identification during check-in to ensure that only registered guests have access to the rooms. Lighting: Well-lit public areas and entrances contribute to the overall safety and security of a hotel. Secure Parking: If the hotel offers parking facilities, having well-lit and secure parking areas can contribute to guest safety.
